28 /*
29  * This function calculates statistics along lines by averaging over time.
30  *
31  * Additionally, averaging in circumferential direction can be performed if desired.
32  *
33  * General assumptions:
34  *
35  * - we assume straight lines and an equidistant distribution of the evaluation points along each
36  * line.
37  *
38  * Assumptions for averaging in circumferential direction:
39  *
40  * - to define the plane in which we want to perform the averaging in circumferential direction,
41  * a normal vector has to be specified that has to be oriented normal to the straight line and
42  * normal to the averaging plane. To construct the sample points for averaging in circumferential
43  * direction, we assume that the first point of the line (line.begin) defines the center of the
44  * circle, and that the other points in circumferential direction can be constructed by rotating
45  * the vector from the center of the circle (line.begin) to the current point along the line
46  * around the normal vector.
47  */
